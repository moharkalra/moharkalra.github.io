VR Camera

When we look upon the world, our brains see symbols. Looking at a tree, I abstract away all of its unique identifying features and assign to it the label “tree”. When I see this tree, I see not that specific tree, but rather my mental image of what “tree” looks like based on all of the trees I have ever seen in my life. In the process, I begin to lose the ability to meaningfully engage with all the diverse visual stimuli in the world around me.

VR Camera forces wearers to manually focus on whatever they want to see, abstracting away depth into Brakhagian planes of 2D visual information parsed through by the wearer’s hand. Transparent LCDs on each eye are connected to an accelerometer and obscure completely if the wearer moves too quickly, disrupting the continuity between what one sees from one vantage point to the next and reframing the world as an alternate reality of camera-made fixed images.

Technical Design:

  • An ESP32 module populates two transparent SSD1309 driven LCD dsiplays with a random pattern of squares on start-up, unpredictably obscuring parts of the wearer's vision to disorient them.
  • The ESP32 reads rotation data from an MPU9250 accelerometer to track the wearer's head movements
  • If the wearer moves their head slowly, the LCD displays scroll inertially. To obtain this effect, part of the Arduino LCD library had to be rewritten to correctly set the LCD registers
  • If the wearer moves their head too quickly, both LCD's become filled with activated pixels for a few seconds and then are reset with a new pattern of squares to disrupt the wearer's mental map of space
